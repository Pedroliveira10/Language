# Curriculum

Each language follows CEFR levels and is divided into units with explicit
learning outcomes. A unit should mix recognition, recall, listening, sentence
building, and guided conversation.

Every exercise needs:

- a globally unique `id`;
- one supported `type`;
- a short, unambiguous `prompt`;
- the data required by that exercise type;
- an answer or completion rule;
- a CEFR-appropriate explanation where useful.

Initial supported types are `multiple-choice`, `manual-input`,
`sentence-order`, `listening`, and `conversation`.
